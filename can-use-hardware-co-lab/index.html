How can I use the HARDWARE.co Lab?

January 18, 2015

We have already had an overwhelmingly positive response from the community regarding the opening of our very first HARDWARE.co Lab on January 20th, 2015.The HARDWARE.co Lab by Conrad at betahaus | Berlin is a resource for the tech community in Berlin regardless of skill level. There are a number of ways to take advantage of this space for HARDWARE.co and betahaus community members. For those of you waiting to see the space and already thinking about how you might use this great resource, we have put together a few ways you can get involved right now.

Here are three ways that people can use the space:

Apply for the HARDWARE.co Accelerator Program

The HARDWARE.co Accelerator program accepts early stage teams and individuals building great hardware products and startups. Over the 14-day free program, startups receive access to 50+ mentors to advise on business and hardware topics. The program culminates in a public demo day to pitch their idea to the community and a number of investors.

For the first time, HARDWARE.co Accelerator Teams will also enjoy full access to the HARDWARE.co Lab facilities as part of the program. This will include full access to the lab as a workspace, as well as contact with the Lab’s team to advise on prototyping, technical manufacturing and testing.

The application process for the accelerator is open until the 28th of January.

Become a Lab Resident

The residency program will be a pay-per-month program for selected residents curated by betahaus. Those who join the program will have full access to the laboratory each day and also receive support and technical input from the highly trained Lab Team. To apply for the lab residency you simply submit a project through the online form on the betahaus website and once accepted, sign up for a full-time membership and desk at the lab.

More at betahaus.com/berlin/spaces/hardwarelab

For beginner or amateur electronics enthusiasts, betahaus will be offering regular courses in circuit design, physical computing and other electronic topics for people looking to improve their skills. All classes are taught by the highly trained Lab Team and can be found in the betahaus program.

More at betahaus.com/berlin/program
